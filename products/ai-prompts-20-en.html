1. Polite Rejection Email

Write a polite email declining the following request.
Maintain the relationship, leave the door open for future opportunities,
but be clear that this specific request is being declined.

[Request being declined]
Example: Invitation to speak at a conference next month

[Reason for declining]
Example: Schedule conflict with a major product launch

[Relationship with recipient]
Example: Industry peer, met at 2 conferences

2. Gentle Reminder/Follow-up

Write a follow-up email about the matter below.
Keep it friendly and non-accusatory. Express understanding
while clearly communicating urgency.

[What you're following up on]
Example: Invoice payment

[When you first reached out]
Example: 2 weeks ago

[Urgency level]
Example: Need resolution by end of week

3. Apology Email

Write an apology email for the situation below.
Be sincere without being overly defensive. Include
what went wrong and how you'll prevent it in the future.

[What you're apologizing for]
Example: Missed deadline by 3 days

[What caused it (if appropriate to share)]
Example: Unexpected technical issues during QA

[Prevention plan]
Example: Adding buffer time to future estimates

4. Cold Outreach Email

Write a cold outreach email to request a meeting.
Keep it short, focus on value to the recipient,
and make it easy to say yes.

[Your role/company]
Example: Founder of a B2B SaaS startup

[Recipient's role]
Example: VP of Marketing at a mid-size e-commerce company

[Purpose of meeting]
Example: Explore potential partnership

[Value proposition for them]
Example: Our tool has helped similar companies reduce CAC by 30%

5. Thank You Email (Memorable)

Write a thank you email that stands out.
Be specific about what you're grateful for and
the impact it had. Avoid generic phrases.

[What you're thanking them for]
Example: Introducing me to their investor network

[Specific impact it had]
Example: Led to 3 meetings and one term sheet
